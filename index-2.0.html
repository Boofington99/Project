About The Trip

This website documents my Digital Media & Design experience in South Korea through photography, journal entries, cultural exploration, and design inspiration.


During the summer of 2026, I had the opportunity to travel to South Korea through the University of Connecticut's Digital Media & Design program. Throughout this month-long experience, I explored Korean culture, design, technology, museums, architecture, and everyday life while documenting my journey through photography and journal entries. This website serves as a collection of my experiences, reflections, and discoveries during my time abroad. From visits to museums and design studios to exploring Seoul's neighborhoods, food, and culture, each entry captures a different aspect of the trip. My goal is to share what I learned, how the experience influenced my perspective as a designer, and the memories I created throughout this unique opportunity.

My Daily Journal

When coming to Korea we were task to create a journal to document our experience. This journal is a collection of my thoughts, feelings, and reflections during my time in South Korea. It captures the highs and lows, the challenges and triumphs, and everything in between. Through this journal, I hope to share my journey and provide insights into the transformative power of travel and cultural immersion.

Early Week - May 11 - May 17

Entire Week wrap up


Getting here early was a great idea. I had the opportunity to explore the area around us and become familiar with the environment. Adjusting emotionally has been more difficult, though. I miss my family, and I have never been this far away from them before. However, being here with my friend has made the transition easier.

I am very excited to start the official week of the school program and meet everyone who will be participating. I am especially looking forward to the welcome dinner. I enjoy trying new foods, so I think this will be a great experience to share with everyone.

Culture

Throughout my time in South Korea, I experienced many cultural differences that highlighted the importance of respect, community, and consideration for others. Public transportation was designed with accessibility in mind, including priority seating areas for pregnant women and older adults. Many public spaces displayed signs encouraging people to remain quiet, helping create a calm environment for everyone. I also noticed how people often stood to one side of escalators and stairways, allowing those in a hurry to pass. Small details like these demonstrated how everyday routines are shaped by a strong awareness of others.



I also learned about the impact of Korean media, technology, and public life. Korea's film industry has become highly influential both domestically and internationally, and it was interesting to see how movies were adapted for Korean audiences, including changes from English text to Korean text within animations. During our stay, I learned that Election Day is treated as an important civic event, with many people receiving time off from work to vote. I also observed safety features throughout the subway system, including platform screen doors that separate passengers from the tracks. These experiences helped me better understand how Korean society balances innovation, safety, and civic responsibility while maintaining strong cultural traditions.



Reflection

Studying abroad in South Korea was one of the most rewarding experiences of my life. Beyond learning about design, culture, and technology, the experience helped me grow as a person. Being away from home for an extended period made me realize how important my friends and family are to me and how much I value the support they provide. While I enjoyed exploring a new country and gaining independence, the experience gave me a deeper appreciation for the relationships I have back home. I returned with new perspectives, lasting memories, and a greater understanding of both another culture and myself.
